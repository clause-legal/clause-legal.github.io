                             PROPRIETARY INFORMATION
                            NON-DISCLOSURE AGREEMENT

This Agreement is made this __day of ____, 1995 by and between Ingram Micro
Inc., a California corporation with its business at 1600 East St. Andrew Place,
Santa Ana, CA 92799-5125 ("Ingram"), and Macromedia, Inc., a California
corporation, with its business at 600 Townsend Street, San Francisco, California
94103 ("Vendor") .

WHEREAS Ingram has compiled and organized certain information relating to its
sales which is proprietary and confidential, known as the "non-standard,
subsection (i), point of sale (POS) data" component of its "Systems Sales Out
Report" ("Proprietary Information"); and

WHEREAS Ingram agrees to disclose Proprietary Information to Vendor for the
limited purpose set out herein; and

WHEREAS Vendor desires to inspect such Proprietary Information so Vendor may
monitor sales through distribution;

NOW, THEREFORE, in consideration of the mutual promises set out herein, the
parties hereby agree as follows:

1. Except as authorized herein, <*$p$*>Vendor agrees not to communicate, disclose, or otherwise make available all or any part of the Proprietary Information to any third party, including Vendor's parent, subsidiaries, or affiliated companies, unless such disclosure is required by law.<*$p$*>

2. <*$p$*>Vendor may use the Proprietary Information for purposes beneficial to Ingram Micro, as reasonably determined by Vendor.<*$p$*> <*$p$*>Vendor agrees to make as many copies of the Proprietary Information as necessary for its business operations; and Vendor agrees to limit distribution of and access to the Proprietary Information to those of Vendor's personnel who deem it necessary.<*$p$*> Vendor agrees not to directly contact,
for the purpose of soliciting, or selling Product directly to any customer or
dealer listed in the Proprietary Information.

3. <*$p$*>Vendor and Ingram mutually agree that all copies of the Proprietary Information and all written descriptions, extractions, or summaries thereof, whether made by Vendor or Ingram, shall be the property of Ingram, unless Vendor believes such information is essential for its continued business operations, in which case Vendor shall retain a copy.<*$p$*>

4. <*$p$*>Vendor and Ingram mutually agree that if Ingram believes there has been a public disclosure of the Proprietary Information, Ingram will decide whether Vendor is released from confidentiality.<*$p$*>

5. <*$p$*>Upon termination of this Agreement by Ingram, Vendor will return the Proprietary Information. If Vendor terminates the agreement, it is under no obligation to return Proprietary Information. Vendor's return obligation shall survive the termination of this Agreement only when terminated by Ingram.<*$p$*>

6. The rights, promises, duties, and obligations set out herein, and the
validity, interpretation, performance, and legal effect of the whole Agreement
shall be governed and determined by the laws of the State of California. <*$p$*>In the event that any provision is found invalid or unenforceable pursuant to statutory or Judicial decree, such provision shall be automatically replaced with a new provision, at Vendor's discretion, that achieves a similar outcome, and the remainder of the Agreement shall be valid and enforceable in accordance with its terms.<*$p$*>
