                             PROPRIETARY INFORMATION
                            NON-DISCLOSURE AGREEMENT

This Agreement is made this __day of ____, 1995 by and between Ingram Micro
Inc., a California corporation with its business at 1600 East St. Andrew Place,
Santa Ana, CA 92799-5125 ("Ingram"), and Macromedia, Inc., a California
corporation, with its business at 600 Townsend Street, San Francisco, California
94103 ("Vendor") .

WHEREAS Ingram has compiled and organized certain information relating to its
sales which is proprietary and confidential, known as the "non-standard,
subsection (i), point of sale (POS) data" component of its "Systems Sales Out
Report" ("Proprietary Information"); and

WHEREAS Ingram agrees to disclose Proprietary Information to Vendor for the
limited purpose set out herein; and

WHEREAS Vendor desires to inspect such Proprietary Information so Vendor may
monitor sales through distribution;

NOW, THEREFORE, in consideration of the mutual promises set out herein, the
parties hereby agree as follows:

<*$p$*>1. Except as authorized herein, Vendor agrees to use reasonable efforts not to communicate, disclose, or otherwise make available all or any part of the Proprietary Information to any third party, including, but not limited to Vendor's parent, subsidiaries, or affiliated companies.<*$p$*>

<*$p$*>2. Vendor agrees to endeavor not to use, or permit others to use, the Proprietary Information, other than for the general purpose of monitoring sales through distribution. Vendor may make a few copies of the Proprietary Information unless otherwise agreed in writing between the parties; and Vendor agrees to generally limit distribution of and access to the Proprietary Information to Vendor's personnel.<*$p$*> Vendor agrees not to directly contact,
for the purpose of soliciting, or selling Product directly to any customer or
dealer listed in the Proprietary Information.

<*$p$*>3. Vendor and Ingram mutually agree that all copies of the Proprietary Information and all written descriptions, extractions, or summaries thereof, whether made by Vendor or Ingram, shall generally be the property of Ingram, and should, upon expiration of this Agreement or Ingram's request, be returned to Ingram in a reasonable timeframe.<*$p$*>

4. Vendor and Ingram mutually agree that Ingram's public disclosure of the
Proprietary Information, except pursuant to a confidential disclosure agreement,
to any party will release Vendor from the obligation of confidentiality with
respect to that portion of the Proprietary Information actually disclosed by
Ingram.

<*$p$*>5. Upon termination of this Agreement by either party for any reason, Vendor shall, if reasonably possible, return all Proprietary Information to Ingram within approximately thirty (30) days, dependent on circumstances.<*$p$*> For purposes of enforcing this provision, Vendor's
return obligation shall survive the termination of this Agreement.

<*$p$*>6. The rights, promises, duties, and obligations set out herein, and the general validity, interpretation, performance, and legal effect of the whole Agreement shall be generally considered to be governed by the laws of the State of California.<*$p$*> In the
event that any provision is found invalid or unenforceable pursuant to
