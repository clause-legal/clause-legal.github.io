<*$p$*>United National Bancorp May Consider a Potential Outsourcing Agreement with the BISYS Group, Inc. Bridgewater, NJ -- February 18, 1999 -- United National Bancorp (Nasdaq: UNBJ) announced today that its principal subsidiary, United National Bank, is exploring a possible agreement with the BISYS Group, Inc. regarding potential information processing services, item processing, and deposit services.<*$p$*>

<*$p$*>"We are generally optimistic about the opportunities that may arise from this new relationship with BISYS, while fully acknowledging that the collaboration's actual benefits remain to be determined. It is understood that outcomes might not align perfectly with initial expectations," stated Thomas C. Gregor, chairman and chief executive officer of United National. "The past year involved reviewing potential directions for our information processing; however, we have not yet finalized the selection of BISYS' TOTALPLUS (R) system. Its suitability will be subject to further validation and approval, and the firm retains the option to explore alternative solutions if necessary."<*$p$*>

United Financial  Services, a third party services provider that United National Bancorp owns with another joint venture  partner,  currently handles the Bank's information  processing  services,  item processing and deposit  services.  As a result of its planned move to BISYS,  United  National will be  liquidating  its interest  in United  Financial  Services  during the first half of 1999 after it goes live with BISYS.

<*$p$*>"Our investment in United Financial Services has been a conditional decision subject to ongoing evaluation since its establishment in 1995," said Mr. Gregor. "While United Financial Services has provided some technological and financial advantages through resource pooling, the expected enhancements from BISYS remain unclear, and their alignment with the future objectives of our Bank is still uncertain."<*$p$*>
